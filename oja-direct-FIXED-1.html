Waitlist Engine

Build 10,000 Pre-Buyers Before Launch

Every person you add to a waitlist before going live is a guaranteed conversion when you flip the switch.

📲
Step 1: TikTok / Reels Hook
Film yourself at a Lagos market. Ask vendor for rice price (₦72,000). Cut to: "Oja-Direct price: ₦53,500. Same rice. Same Saturday." 15 seconds. Post daily for 7 days.
💬
Step 2: WhatsApp Waitlist
CTA on every video: "Join your state's group. Send your state name to our WhatsApp." Manually add to a waitlist Google Sheet. Build to 500 per state before going live.
🎯
Step 3: Captain Blast
Once you have 5 confirmed State Captains, each blasts the Oja-Direct platform link to their existing neighborhood WhatsApp groups. One Captain × 500 members = instant buyers per state.
💳
Step 4: First Live Payment
When a neighborhood group hits 20 members, open the Paystack payment link on this platform. Collect payment. Place wholesale order. You just made your first 10%.
💬 WhatsApp Cloud API — The Upgrade You Must Make at Scale
At 1,000 customers/week, a regular phone number will get banned by Meta for high message volume. Before you hit 500 orders, upgrade to the WhatsApp Cloud API (free via Meta for Business). This runs a proper AI bot — not a phone.
1
Register at business.facebook.com — Create a Meta Business Account and verify your business phone number as a WhatsApp Business API number.
2
Build with n8n or Make.com (free tier) — Set up automation flows: "User texts state name → bot replies with their state's open group buy link + current price." No coding required.
3
Add Smart Regional Routing — When a user in Enugu texts, bot automatically checks South East hub progress, not Lagos. One bot handles all 37 states automatically.
4
Paystack Webhook Integration — The moment a payment is confirmed, the bot auto-sends a receipt + delivery schedule to the customer. Zero manual work from you.
🌡️ Phase 3: Cold Chain Integration — The Billion-Naira Unlock
Once your dry goods model is stable (500+ orders/week), connect to Nigeria's National Cold Chain Policy infrastructure. Tomatoes, peppers, onions, and fresh produce have 10x higher demand than rice — and 90% spoilage without cold chain. You solve both problems simultaneously.
Phase 3A
Dry Goods (Now)
Rice, beans, oil, garri. No cold chain needed. Proven model already running.
Phase 3B
Produce (6–12 months)
Tomatoes, peppers, onions. Partner with Agility cold storage. Saturday delivery within 4hr pickup windows.
Phase 3C
Protein (12–18 months)
Fish, chicken, frozen goods. Use Port Harcourt hub's seafood network. Higher margins, higher demand.

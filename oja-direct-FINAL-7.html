💡 Note: Logistics (truck + delivery) is included in the wholesaler's 75%. They own, pack and deliver the goods. 75 + 20 + 5 = 100% — every naira accounted for.
🤔
What exactly is Oja-Direct?
Think of it like a buying club. Instead of one person buying a bag of rice from the market, 20–50 neighbors buy together. Because the order is big, the farmer sells cheaper. You are the one who organizes this — and you take a 20% fee for doing so.
💡 Analogy: You're like the organizer of a "bulk buy trip" to a warehouse. Everyone pays you their share, you buy in bulk and earn a cut.
💰
How do I actually earn money?
Every time a customer pays ₦60,000 for rice, ₦12,000 automatically goes to your wallet via Paystack. You never touch the food, never drive a truck. The platform handles payment, the wholesaler handles goods, the captain handles delivery. You get paid automatically.
💡 Example: 10 orders in one day = ₦120,000 in your pocket. 100 orders/week = ₦1,200,000/week.
🏘️
What is a "State Captain"?
A Captain is a local person (like an estate secretary or cooperative leader) who finds buyers in their neighborhood. They share the platform link on WhatsApp, collect orders, and coordinate Saturday delivery. They earn 5% per order. You earn 20%. They do the local work. You built the system.
💡 Think of Captains like your local sales agents — they bring customers, you split the profit.
📦
Do I need to store food or own a truck?
No. You own none of the goods. You never touch the food. The wholesaler owns and delivers the goods. You own the platform — the website, the payment flow, the customer relationships. You make money by connecting people, not by holding inventory.
💡 You're like an Airbnb host who owns the app — not the houses. The landlords (wholesalers) do the physical work.
📱
How do customers find and pay?
Customers visit this website, click their state, find their neighborhood group, enter their details, and pay via Paystack (card, bank transfer, OPay, USSD). Everything is online. No cash handling, no manual receipts. Paystack sends them a receipt automatically and splits the money to everyone involved.
💡 Your job is just to bring people to the website. Once they're here, the platform does the rest.
🎯
How long before I earn my first ₦100,000?
If you get 2 State Captains and each has 30 customers ordering weekly, that's 60 orders × ₦12,000 = ₦720,000 profit per week. Your first ₦100k comes within the first week of real orders. The 100-Day Plan on the Growth page shows you exactly how to get there.
💡 Focus on Week 1: Get ONE Captain. Help them get 20 buyers. That's ₦240,000 your first week.

📖 Business Glossary
Group Buy
When 20–50 households pool their orders together. Because the total order is huge, the farm sells at a much cheaper "wholesale" price — the same price a supermarket pays.
State Captain
A local community leader (like an estate secretary or cooperative head) who finds buyers in their area, shares your payment link, and coordinates Saturday delivery. They earn 5% of every order automatically.
Paystack
Nigeria's biggest and most trusted payment company (like Stripe). It handles all the money — holding it safely until delivery is confirmed, then automatically splitting it to the wholesaler, you, and the Captain.
Escrow
A safe holding account. When a customer pays, the money doesn't go straight to the wholesaler — it sits in Paystack's escrow until the goods are delivered. If delivery fails, the money automatically comes back to the customer.
Wholesale Price
The price a supermarket or big buyer pays for goods — much lower than what you see in the market. By grouping 20–50 households together, Oja-Direct gets you this same bulk discount.
Platform Fee (20%)
Your cut as the platform owner. On every ₦60,000 order, ₦12,000 goes automatically to your Paystack account. You didn't touch the food, drive the truck, or speak to the customer — you just own the system.
Regional Hub
A large warehouse in each zone (Kano for the North, Lagos for the South-West, etc.) where food is bulk-stored and packaged before being dispatched to different estates on Saturdays.
GMV (Gross Merchandise Value)
The total value of all sales on the platform. If 100 people each buy ₦60,000 of rice, the GMV is ₦6,000,000. Your profit is 20% of GMV = ₦1,200,000.
Last-Mile Delivery
The final part of delivery — from the warehouse to your estate gate. This is what the Captain coordinates: confirming gate access, truck timing, and ensuring each buyer picks up their order.
Wave (1, 2, 3)
The rollout phases. Wave 1 = the 5 biggest cities launched first (Lagos, Abuja, Kano, Port Harcourt, Ibadan). Wave 2 = 5 more regional capitals. Wave 3 = all 36 states + FCT.
